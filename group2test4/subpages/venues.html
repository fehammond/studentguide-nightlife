Pubs

One of the well known pastimes of students is drinking. The UK has tonnes of pubs, all looking for someone to claim them as their local. Manchester has an array of outstanding pubs and bars.

An example is The Wharf. This large, warehouse style pub on the banks of Canal Basin. This large pub has many cosy spots for you to relax in and have a drink or two, with a lively atmosphere and bites to eat. The pub includes Vegetarian Friendly, Vegan Options and Gluten Free Options. Another pub to keep an eye on is Sinclairs Oyster Bar. This pub is located in Exchange square, right in the heart of Manchester's busiest shopping centres. This building has been around since the 1600s, and its appearance clearly reflects such an era. This is a great place to go for a drink after a long day of shopping! Finally, we have City Arms. This 190 year old pub houses many real ales, craft beers and ciders. Its a small location but houses a beer garden and has traditional pub games to keep you occupied while having a drink and a relax.

Clubs

Students are known to enjoy clubbing, partying and having a great night out with friends. This favoured activity has not gone unnoticed in Manchester, and many music scenes were started in the city.

One pub with great atmosphere is Eden. This multi-storey establishment hosts floors of different styles and feels. The top floors itself has three different club spaces, each with a separate atmosphere. As well as this, it boasts its own on site karaoke. Another great club space is South. South hosts Local DJs and has been since 1995. This club has underground vibes, playing mixes of 90s style music that include Rock and Roll and House. Finally, one of our more exclusive clubs is The Warehouse Project. Beginning in 2006, this club is based in an industrial warehouse in Manchester's outskirts. However, this club is only open from from September to New Year's Day each year.

Bars

If you're not in the mood to go out clubbing, how about a simple drink? Manchester has waves of great bars and eateries for you to explore.

Hard Rock Cafe has been around since the 2000s, opening in Manchester from the American chains. This bar and restaurant is home to an amazing atmosphere, with live sport and live music being shown every night.. It also includes a selection of alcoholic drinks, including its own happy hour and signature drinks. Located in the Printworks, it is always busy but hosts an amazing atmosphere. Another location for those looking to upscale their evening is The Alchemist. This bar is host to Master Mixologists, offering bookings for "experiences" of their creations. The premise of this evening may entice you into wondering, but the experiences are usually rarely the same. Finally, if you are looking for something new, how about Cane And Grain? This American-inspired bar has its own upstairs space for cocktails and other drinks of choice like bourbon and beer. Known to be "carnivorous AND boozy", this location is great for a different experience at a great price.

Gaming & Drinking

However, if you're not a fan of sitting down, there are many other places in Manchester to have a great evening out.

If you enjoy movies, look no further than The Printworks, home to Vue cinema. This cheap but high quality cinema experience is bound to leave you mesmerised. There are many different films and screens to choose from, and all are guaranteed to leave you on the edge of your seat, laughing or crying. This is a popular spot amongst students as it has a student discount on tickets. If you want a little more action, Breakout Manchester has you covered. With two locations in Manchester, there are tonnes of rooms to enjoy with friends and family. Test your limits and abilities while having a great night out! Finally, with three courses to choose from, Junkyard golf is your one stop for adventure and a drink to boot. These indoor games will leave you and your group in stitches, especially if you get a few drinks involved. Each course has a slightly different feel, with aesthetic choices being their main grab.
