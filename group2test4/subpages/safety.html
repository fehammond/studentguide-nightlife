Safety in the City

Manchester City is well-known for its nightlife. Students, young adults, tourists from all over the North... it doesn't matter who you are or what your background is, Manchester has something to offer to anyone. Everyone loves a good night out with friends, but a priority is staying safe and avoiding any danger. When planning a night out, it is important to stay safe, under any circumstances.

Security

Greater Manchester Police: the main job of the police is to keep people secure. GMP can be found doing police patrols at night everywhere in the city. Don't be scared, if you require help, approach a car and ask to be protected. If you need urgent help, please call 999.

Greater Manchester Police

Manchester Street Angels are a volunteer group supported by the Christian Nightlife Initiative. They operate around Manchester City Centre's streets during weekend nights. They assure safety in the city along with the others volunteer groups.

Manchester Street Angels

Useful Mobile Apps

Some mobile applications can help you assure more safety: one is 'SafeZone' and the other is 'Hollie Guard'.

Safe Zone is a free to download app offered by Manchester Metropolitan University. You can check–in 24/7, so you can tell MMU Staff your position whenever you feel like it; it offers a quick and discreet call for assistance if first aid is needed or in case of an emergency.

Safe Zone App

Hollie Guard is a personal app that provides live notification if you are in danger 24/7. When used in case of an emergency, the app will call the closest police patrol and/or private security and alert them of your danger.

Hollie Guard

Tips

  • Look out for each other when you’ve had a few drinks and make sure you walk home with friends
  • Plan your journey home before you go out
  • Don't walk home alongside the water after a night out, find a better route home
  • Make sure your friends get home safely after a night out. Don’t let them walk by the water
  • Some bars may have a secret password for asking for help. For example, you can 'ask for Angela' if you feel in danger when clubbing in Canal Street
